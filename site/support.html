Getting started

1. Grant Home access

Home Floorplan needs Apple Home/HomeKit permission to read and control compatible accessories.

2. Open your Home

Select your active Home, then review rooms, accessories, scenes, and automations.

3. Build a floorplan

Common questions

Why do I need HomeKit permission?

The app uses Apple Home/HomeKit to display rooms, accessories, scenes, automations, and states. Without Home access, core smart home controls are unavailable.

Why are some screens empty?

Some features require compatible accessories or sensors. For example, environmental dashboards need compatible temperature, humidity, air quality, occupancy, or similar HomeKit sensors.

Do HomeKit automations continue if the app is closed?

Automations saved to Apple Home run through Apple Home/HomeKit. Live dashboards, local context, and AI suggestions are best tested while Home Floorplan is open.

Why does a curtain or cover open in the wrong direction?

Some window covering devices report open and closed positions differently. Open the accessory control panel and enable Reverse open/close for that accessory only.

Are AI features required?

No. AI features are optional. If no AI provider or API key is configured, HomeKit and floorplan features should continue to work.

Report a problem

When contacting support, include:

  • Your device model and iPadOS/iOS version.
  • The Home Floorplan app version and build number.
  • What you expected to happen and what happened instead.
  • Whether the same accessory behaves correctly in the Apple Home app.
  • Screenshots or screen recordings when helpful.

Email support

Before publishing, replace the support email with a real address that you can monitor.

App Store review information
